Step 1: Your Mortal Identity

Before you became a monster, you were a person. What was your name?

Step 2: Mortal Skills

What were you exceptionally good at in your human life? List three. (e.g., "Blacksmithing", "Charming", "Spear Fighting", "Herbalism")

Step 3: Resources

What valuable physical items or properties do you possess from your human years? List three. (e.g., "A fine horse", "A hidden cave", "Bag of gold coins", "Family signet ring")

Step 4: Mortal Characters

Name three mortals intimately tied to your life — relatives, friends, lovers, enemies, mentors. (e.g., "Eliza, my sister", "Marcus, the local priest"). The immortal who turns you comes later, in Step 8.

Step 5: Memory 1 — Your Mortal Life

Start your first Memory with one broad Experience summarizing who you were before unlife. (e.g. Theme: 'Mortal Life', Experience: 'I am Henri, son of Jon, a poor knight swindled out of my inheritance near the Loire.')

Step 6: Memories 2 & 3

Create two more Experiences, each in its own Memory, each combining two of your traits. (e.g. "Gundar takes me on my first voyage aboard the Longship Bøkesuden; his touch calms me when we first leave sight of land.")

Step 7: Memory 4

One more Experience in its own Memory, again combining two of your traits.

Step 8: The Turning

Lastly, create the Immortal who gifted (or cursed) you with unlife, the Mark it left, and the Memory of your transformation (Memory 5). (e.g. Sire: 'Baron Hollmueller, an Austrian vampire'; Mark: 'My neck is permanently broken'; Experience: 'I duel the eerie Baron across the abbey roof; he nearly takes my head, but I do not die.')
